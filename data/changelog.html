February 13, 2017

Bug fixes

  • Fixed a bug where editor text markers (such as color pickers) stuck around after the line it's on has been folded out of sight.
  • Fixed a bug where these text markers would not appear when lines above them are folded.
  • Fixed a bug where you wouldn’t be shown as signed in if you sign out and then sign back in again in the same session.
  • (Mapzen platform improvement!) Sign-in window now closes automatically after you've signed in. If it doesn't, you should be seeing a button that manually closes the popup window.

February 7, 2017

Tangram Play is now in public beta!

We’re excited to release the public beta of Tangram Play, a text editor for working with Tangram, our web map rendering library. Your use of Play during this phase will help us improve our tools for web map design.

If you're new here, find out more about Tangram Play on the Mapzen blog. The walkthrough gives a brief explanation of the interface, whether you’re creating from a blank slate or using one of our Mapzen basemaps.

If you’ve been here before, welcome back! There's been a few changes to the interface that we’ll describe below.

New features

  • Save a scene to your Mapzen account! Now you can access your scenes from anywhere. With this improvement, we’ve replaced the ability to save to an anonymous GitHub Gist, but if you've saved an anonymous Gist in the past, you can still load those, and save them again to get it into your Mapzen account.
  • Share your scenes. Once you've saved your scene, we also host it for you. Now there’s an easy way to share it with friends or link it from your website or social media!

Let us know what you think: Give your feedback by emailing us at tangram@mapzen.com.
